🛡️ STAY SAFE: Urban Hazard Guide

This guide tells you how to stay safe around common problems in the city.

1️⃣ Potholes & Broken Roads

The Danger: You can trip, fall, or crash your bike or car.

How to stay safe: Always look at the ground while walking. Don’t drive through deep puddles because a big hole might be hiding under the water.

What to do: If you hit a hole while driving, pull over safely to check your tires. Do not swerve suddenly into other cars.

2️⃣ Water Logging (Flooding)

The Danger: Deep water can wash you away or stop your car engine.

How to stay safe: Never walk or drive into water if you cannot see the ground. Even shallow moving water is dangerous.

What to do: If your car gets stuck in rising water, get out immediately and move to higher ground.

3️⃣ Exposed Wires & Fire

The Danger: Electric shocks can be deadly. Fires spread fast.

How to stay safe: Stay far away from loose or hanging wires. If you smell burning, alert others immediately.

What to do: If a wire falls on your car, stay inside. If fire forces you out, jump with both feet together.

4️⃣ Open Manholes

The Danger: Falling into deep holes or inhaling toxic gases.

How to stay safe: Watch your steps, especially at night. Place objects nearby to warn others.

What to do: If someone falls in, do NOT enter. Call emergency services.

5️⃣ Garbage Dumps

The Danger: Germs, rats, and diseases.

How to stay safe: Avoid walking near trash piles. Wear fully covered footwear.

What to do: Wash hands thoroughly if you touch garbage.

6️⃣ Under Construction Areas

The Danger: Falling objects and heavy machinery.

How to stay safe: Obey warning signs and barricades.

What to do: Move away quickly if you hear loud crashes and protect your head.
